Course Description

This course explores advanced topics in social psychology, focusing on how individuals think, influence, and relate to one another. Topics include group dynamics, persuasion, social cognition, attitude change, and the psychological foundations of social interaction.

Students will critically examine contemporary research and apply theories to real-world scenarios, exploring the intersections of psychology and social behavior. By the end of the course, students will develop a deeper understanding of human social behavior and the ways in which psychological principles manifest in everyday life.

Course Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to: 1. Analyze and interpret key theories of social psychology. 2. Evaluate research findings in the context of social behavior. 3. Apply social psychological concepts to real-world situations and case studies. 4. Understand the influence of group dynamics, social cognition, and cultural factors on behavior.

Grading and Evaluation

  • Assignments: 30% (Weekly reflection papers and case study analyses)
  • Midterm Exam: 25% (Week 7)
  • Final Project: 25% (Presentation and Paper)
  • Final Exam: 20% (Week 15)

Grading Scale: - A: 90-100% - B: 80-89% - C: 70-79% - D: 60-69% - F: <60%

Assignments and Projects

  • Weekly Reflections: Students will write short reflection papers (500-700 words) based on the weekly readings, due every Friday.
  • Case Study Analysis: Every other week, students will analyze real-world case studies through the lens of social psychology concepts.
  • Final Project: A research-based project where students present a contemporary social issue through the lens of social psychology, culminating in a presentation and a final paper (2,000-2,500 words).

Course Policies

  • Attendance: Regular attendance is expected. More than 3 unexcused absences will result in a 10% deduction from the final grade.
  • Late Work: Late assignments will incur a penalty of 10% per day, up to a maximum of 3 days. After 3 days, late work will not be accepted unless previously arranged with the instructor.
  • Academic Integrity: Plagiarism and cheating are strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action in accordance with university policies.
